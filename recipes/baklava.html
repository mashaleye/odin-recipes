Baklava!

A delicious phyllo pastry popular in Middle Eastern countries.
Its supposed origins are Turkish, dating to the Byzantine Empire (or even further),
though many cultures claim it for their own. Many Greek and Lebanese
restaurants serve it, and it is now a featured dessert of several former Ottoman countries.

Ingredients

    Baklava

  • 1 pound chopped nuts (almonds, walnuts, or pistachios are best, or use
    a combination of them)
  • 1 pound phyllo dough, thawed
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/3 teaspoon ground cloves

    Syrup

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Finely ground pistachios for garnish, optional
